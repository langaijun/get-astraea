About Artemis

Artemis is the goddess of the hunt, wild animals, wilderness, and the moon. She swore eternal virginity to maintain her independence and freedom. Artemis represents the untamed aspects of nature and the power of self-reliance. In modern life, her energy appears in those who value their independence, protect boundaries, and find strength in solitude.

In Mythology

Artemis is the daughter of Zeus and Leto, twin sister of Apollo. She roamed the forests with her band of nymphs, hunting and protecting the wild. She was fiercely protective of her maidens and punished those who threatened them. The deer and cypress are sacred to her, and she carries a silver bow.

Who Artemis Chooses

Artemis may be your guiding deity if you feel most at home in nature, value your independence fiercely, have strong protective instincts, and prefer solitude over crowds. You may find yourself naturally defending others and setting clear boundaries.
